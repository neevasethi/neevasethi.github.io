About

Hi, I'm Neeva.

I spend my days making large systems intelligible & zooming out to see how infrastructure, incentives, and human decisions quietly shape who gets access, scale, and power.

My work lives at the intersection of technology, scale, and societal impact. As a software engineer at Microsoft, I've helped scale high-performance and AI infrastructure that underpins some of the world's most demanding workloads. Earlier, my path took me through biology, medicine-adjacent research, and health-tech projects, all of which are experiences that clarified a central conviction: meaningful progress in healthcare isn't just about innovation, but about how systems are designed, governed, and deployed at scale.

Outside of work, I write and think about ambition, identity, and how people articulate what they care about, often discovering that clarity emerges through conversation itself. Travel grounds me in this curiosity; moving through new places reminds me that despite different languages, laws, and landscapes, we're all navigating the same human questions.

Across everything, I'm drawn to building bridges: between logic and intuition, engineering and ethics, individual action and systemic change. I'm energized by people who think expansively, move with intention, and believe generosity is a form of leadership. If any of this resonates, I'd love to talk about what you're building, questioning, or trying to change.
